Luqi Sun

I am currently a master’s student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, working as a Research Assistant in the SMILE Lab affiliated with the Center for Language and Speech Processing (CLSP), under the supervision of Professor Berrak Sisman. I received my bachelor’s degree from Dalian University of Technology, where I worked as a Research Assistant in a laboratory under the supervision of Professor Qiufen Xia during my undergraduate studies.
I am passionate about research. During my undergraduate studies, my research focused on cold-start latency optimization in cloud computing and serverless computing, and I published related research as the first author. Currently, my research interests focus on speech processing and healthcare-related speech detection based on deep learning methods.
In addition to research, I also love films and traveling. My current dream is to travel to France to watch films at the Cannes Film Festival.

Research Experience

The Optimization of Cold Start Latency in Serverless Computing

Research Assistant in Dalian University of Technology, Advised by Prof. Qiufen Xia

Proposed and implemented an innovative Serverless cold-start optimization solution based on nested containers, enabling rapid initialization of child containers through parent-container pre-warming and significantly reducing function cold-start latency. Independently designed and optimized a nested-container resource scheduling system, including parent-container pre-warming strategies to minimize startup delay and resource waste. Independently developed a child-container resource allocation algorithm to improve function execution performance, and built an adaptive Serverless resource scheduling system.
